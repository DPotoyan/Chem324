Atomic Orbitals

What you will learn

  • A wavefunction for a one-electron system is called an orbital. For an atomic system such as the hydrogen atom, it is called an atomic orbital.

  • The orbital plots demonstrate the shapes of the orbitals, but this does not tell us anything about the radial extent (i.e., how far the orbital reaches).

  • As the value of ZZZ is increased, the radial extent decreases. This indicates that for higher nuclear charge the electrons reside closer to the nucleus.

  • The radial wavefunctions have n−l−1n - l - 1n−l−1 nodes between distances of zero and infinity.

  • The angular part of the wavefunctions has lll nodes.

  • The total number of nodes is n−1n - 1n−1.

Definition of an atomic orbital

  • Atomic orbital (AO): a single-electron wavefunction (exact for H-like atoms)

ψnℓmℓ(r,θ,ϕ)=Rnℓ(r) Yℓmℓ(θ,ϕ)\psi_{n\ell m_\ell}(r,\theta,\phi) = R_{n\ell}(r)\,Y_{\ell m_\ell}(\theta,\phi)ψnℓmℓ​​(r,θ,ϕ)=Rnℓ​(r)Yℓmℓ​​(θ,ϕ)
(1)

  • Principal quantum number: n=1,2,3,…n = 1, 2, 3, \ldotsn=1,2,3,…

  • Orbital angular momentum: ℓ=0,1,2,…,n−1\ell = 0, 1, 2, \ldots, n-1ℓ=0,1,2,…,n−1

  • Magnetic quantum number: mℓ=−ℓ,−ℓ+1,…,ℓm_\ell = -\ell, -\ell + 1, \ldots, \ellmℓ​=−ℓ,−ℓ+1,…,ℓ

3D shapes of orbitals¶

  • For degenerate states with l>0l > 0l>0, we have an additional degree of freedom in choosing how to represent the orbitals. In fact, any linear combination of a given set of 2l+12l + 12l+1 orthogonal eigenfunctions corresponding to a degenerate set with orbital angular momentum lll is also a solution to the Schrödinger equation.

  • Two commonly used representations are the Cartesian form, which is real-valued (and, in the case of l=1l = 1l=1, denoted pxp_xpx​, pyp_ypy​, and pzp_zpz​), and the eigenfunctions of angular momentum (L^2\hat{L}^2L^2 and L^z\hat{L}_zL^z​), which are complex-valued and denoted p−1p_{-1}p−1​, p0p_0p0​, and p+1p_{+1}p+1​. pz=p0{p_z = p_0}pz​=p0​

Problems¶

Problem 1: Counting states for n=3n = 3n=3

  • For the hydrogen atom, how many possible quantum states correspond to the principal quantum number n=3n = 3n=3?

  • What are the energies of these states?

  • Consider several values of nnn and show that the number of orbitals for each nnn is n2n^2n2.

Solution:

If n=3n = 3n=3, the allowed values of lll are 0, 1, and 2.

  • If l=0l = 0l=0, then m=0m = 0m=0 (1 state).

  • If l=1l = 1l=1, then m=−1,0,1m = -1, 0, 1m=−1,0,1 (3 states).

  • If l=2l = 2l=2, then m=−2,−1,0,1,2m = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2m=−2,−1,0,1,2 (5 states).

In total there are 1+3+5=91 + 3 + 5 = 91+3+5=9 allowed states. This confirms that the number of orbitals for the H-atom is n2n^2n2.

Because the total energy depends only on the principal quantum number n=3n = 3n=3, the energy of each of these states is:

En=−13.6⋅1n2 [eV]E_n = -13.6 \cdot \frac{1}{n^2} \, [\text{eV}]En​=−13.6⋅n21​[eV]
(13)
Problem 2: Quantum numbers of a 3d3d3d electron

  • The notation 3d3d3d specifies the quantum numbers for an electron in the hydrogen atom. What are the values of nnn and lll?

  • What are the values of the energy and the angular momentum?

  • What are the possible values of the magnetic quantum number?

  • What are the possible orientations of the angular momentum vector?

Solution:

  • The 3d3d3d orbital corresponds to n=3n = 3n=3 and l=2l = 2l=2. The energy is E3=−13.6/9≈−1.51E_3 = -13.6/9 \approx -1.51E3​=−13.6/9≈−1.51 eV and the magnitude of the angular momentum is L=ℏl(l+1)=ℏ6L = \hbar\sqrt{l(l+1)} = \hbar\sqrt{6}L=ℏl(l+1)​=ℏ6​.

  • The possible values of the magnetic quantum number are ml=−2,−1,0,1,2m_l = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2ml​=−2,−1,0,1,2 (five values).

  • The orientation of LzL_zLz​ with respect to LLL is fixed by cos⁡θ=Lz/L=ml/l(l+1)\cos\theta = L_z / L = m_l / \sqrt{l(l+1)}cosθ=Lz​/L=ml​/l(l+1)​, giving five allowed orientations of the angular momentum vector.

Problem 3: Nodes of the 3pz3p_z3pz​ orbital

Locate the nodes (and nodal surfaces) of the 3pz3p_z3pz​ orbital:

ψ3pz=1812π(1a0)3/2(6ra0−r2a02)e−r/3a0cos⁡(θ)\psi_{3p_z} = \frac{1}{81}\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi}}\left(\frac{1}{a_0}\right)^{3/2}\left(6\frac{r}{a_0} - \frac{r^2}{a_0^2}\right) e^{-r/3a_0}\cos(\theta)ψ3pz​​=811​π2​​(a0​1​)3/2(6a0​r​−a02​r2​)e−r/3a0​cos(θ)
(14)
Solution:

  • 3pz3p_z3pz​ corresponds to n=3n = 3n=3, l=1l = 1l=1, and ml=0m_l = 0ml​=0.

  • The radial part is zero at r=0r = 0r=0 and r=6a0r = 6a_0r=6a0​. The first is part of the boundary condition and not a node, because the wavefunction does not change sign there. Hence there is 1 radial node.

  • The angular part has one nodal surface from cos⁡θ=0\cos\theta = 0cosθ=0, which occurs at θ=π2\theta = \frac{\pi}{2}θ=2π​ (the xyxyxy-plane).

  • We thus have 2 nodes for the 3pz3p_z3pz​ orbital. The total number of nodes is n−1n - 1n−1, made up of lll angular nodes and n−l−1n - l - 1n−l−1 radial nodes.
