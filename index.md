# Quantum Mechanics for Chemistry

Welcome to **Chem 3240: Quantum Mechanics for Chemistry**, Fall 2026.

These notes follow the historical path into quantum theory: from the puzzles of
classical physics through the birth of quantization, wave-particle duality, and the
Schrödinger equation, building toward the quantum description of atoms and molecules.

:::{note}
These notes are written to be read alongside lecture, and they contain runnable Python demos: move a slider and the plots recompute in your browser. Pages with the marimo button also open as editable notebooks. Computational projects live on the [Projects page](projects/00-computational-projects.md).
:::

## How to use these notes

- **Lecture pages** carry the core theory, figures, and worked examples.
- **Demo pages** carry interactive Python you can run and modify right on the page.
- **Projects** are graded notebooks you complete in the cloud and submit on Canvas.

Start with [Chapter 1](ch01/01-why-quantum-mechanics.md).
