# Practice Problems

:::{note} **What you will learn**

- **A set of practice problems for Exam 2** spanning the model systems and foundations of quantum mechanics.
- **Topics covered include** the harmonic oscillator, the particle in a box (1D and 3D), expectation values, degeneracy, Hermitian operators, and superposition states.
- **Work through these to consolidate** the postulates and model systems before the exam.
:::

### Problems

:::{note} **Problem 1**
Compute the average momentum of the harmonic oscillator.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 2**
Compute the average position of an electron confined in a 1D box of size $L$.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 3**
How would the degeneracy of the first excited state of a 3D cubic box change when you increase the $z$ dimension two-fold?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 4**
Calculate the energy of a harmonic oscillator in its ground state given spring constant $k = 12 \, \text{N/m}$ and mass $0.5 \, \text{kg}$.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 5**
Determine the probability density function of a particle in a box of length $L$ in the first excited state $n=2$ at a point $x = \frac{L}{4}$.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 6**
Determine the wavelength of the photon needed to excite an electron from the ground state to the first excited state in a 1D box of length $L = 1 \, \text{nm}$.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 7**
Calculate the average kinetic energy of a particle in a box given that it is found 90% of the time in the ground state and 10% of the time in the first excited state.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 8**
Write down the time-dependent wave function for the particle in a box of the previous problem.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 9**
Write down the explicit integral for computing the probability of an electron being found in the left third of the box.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 10**
Which of the following operators are not Hermitian: $x$, $x^2$, $-i\frac{d}{dx}$, $-i\frac{d^2}{dx^2}$, $\frac{d}{dx}$?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 11**
You made a measurement of two components of momentum and found their values. What can we say about the operators corresponding to these observables?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 12**
What is $\langle 1|\psi\rangle$ equal to for a quantum state described by $|\psi\rangle$ that is characterized by a superposition of the first four states of the harmonic oscillator with equal probabilities?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 13**
Which ground state of an electron will be lower if it is placed in a box with $L_x = 2L_y = 3L_z$ versus $L_x = 3L_y = 2L_z$?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 14**
Calculate the energy of a 3D symmetric harmonic oscillator in its ground state. Take $\hbar\omega = 1$ for all dimensions.
:::

:::{note} **Problem 15**
Given the operator $\frac{d}{dx}$ and its two eigenfunctions $|0\rangle$ and $|1\rangle$, will $\langle 0|1\rangle$ be zero?
:::

:::{note} **Problem 16**
In units of $m=1$, $h=1$, $L=1$, the 3D particle in a box has energy $E = 112$. Determine the degeneracy of the energy level and list all the quantum numbers.
:::
